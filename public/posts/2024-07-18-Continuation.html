Yesterday's post came on a little strong I think, but it still set a good foundation for what I hope to use this blog for. It was late at night when I was writing that post, as I was unable to sleep because I had a cup of coffee that morning.

I have a strange relationship with coffee and caffeine in general. I suspect that I may have ADHD, but caffeine doesn't seem to put me down like it does for many ADHDers. Instead, it has the typical effect of increased focus, jitters, and inability to sleep. However, it has some strange side effects. I've noticed that on days in which I have coffee (never after noon and only one cup) I tend to get a sort of sensory overload headache around 5pm that lasts until I eventually fall asleep. This sensory overload was initially mistaken for a form of misophonia, as my father has the condition and I suspected I had the same. Indeed, many symptoms are the same, with increased irritability to people talking or small noises but my brain also just generally feels like it is under attack, and the headache portion of it is overwhelming and mentally deafening.

"Just give up caffeine, then." The problem is that caffeine has a give along with this take. This heightened sensory state can be manipulated to my advantage if my space is quiet and regulated. Several times I have used it to trigger a state of hyperfocus toward whatever obsession I am working on at the time, during which I can accomplish seemingly superhuman amounts of work over the course of several hours.

When I was in high school I was drinking coffee or energy drinks daily, and I built up a tolerance to the negative side effects mentioned above, but also unfortunately the same was true of the positive effects.

Now I typically use it as a tool, saving it for the once or twice a month in which I'd like a greater chance of triggering a state of hyperfocus or am in dire need of help staying awake. This results in some days in which I have coffee and am unable to keep a clean, quiet environment, and overstimulation leads to a state of angry, energetic outbursts as I attempt to get my brain to shut up for a second.

This was the state in which I wrote last night's post, and contributed to the overall tone and aggresiveness of my writing. Eventually I hope to be on top of my sleep schedule enough that I will not have to rely on caffeine as often, and can avoid these intermittent side effects (and the following sleep loss) whenever possible.
